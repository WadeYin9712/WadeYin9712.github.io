I am a 1st-year PhD student in Computer Science at UCLA advised by Prof. Kai-Wei Chang. I am a member of UCLANLP lab, and working closely with Prof. Nanyun Peng at UCLA. During my first year, I enjoyed great collaboration with Prof. Dragomir Radev at Yale University.

I study Artificial Intelligence (AI), including Natural Language Processing (NLP) and Computer Vision (CV). Specifically, my research interest is knowledge acquisition and building knowledge-enriched models, which aims at mining heterogeneous knowledge from various domains and modalities, and organically integrating extracted knowledge with neural network models via controllable and interpretable methods.

Before coming to UCLA, I completed my bachelor's at Peking University, where I worked with Prof. Xiaojun Wan and Prof. Baobao Chang.
